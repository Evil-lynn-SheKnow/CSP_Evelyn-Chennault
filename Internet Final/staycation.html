Zion National Park

Geography

Zion is best described as a landscape dominated by mountains, canyons, and rivers. It is famously known to be “carved” by the Virgin River. The colorful red rocks are what are classified as Navajo Sandstone. This rock is brittle in some cases and solid in others. Beautiful stripes and patterns make up the canyon, followed by several waterways and various kinds of wildlife. As a National Park, it is visited by people all over the world. Unlike other National Parks in Utah, Zion is a canyon with many narrow passages, which contributes to the hikes and tours given. Zion also has been rated for its really beautiful sunrises, sunsets, and viewable star formations.

Tourist Attractions

National parks are already known for their gorgeous colors within the rocks, but there is another beauty that sandstone gives to us: structure. The most common and beautiful places to visit are the hiking trails, river rafting, and rock climbing. See the lists below for own plans for your vacation!

Most Popular

  • Navigating the Narrows, the narrowest section of East Zion
  • Walking Pa'rus Trail, a simple hike that does not require much effort
  • Emerald Pools Trail, a walk that is known for beautiful water features
  • Canyon Overlook Trail, a trail that leads to breathaking views of the park
  • West Rim Trail
  • Angels Landing
  • Kayaking Virgin River
  • Climbing Lamb's Knoll
  • Exploring East Zion area
